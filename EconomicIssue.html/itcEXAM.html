Inflation, Explained: Causes of Increasing in the Philippines

Inflation signifies a gradual increase in prices over time, serving as a significant economic gauge
with far-reaching consequences for both businesses and investors. Notably, the Philippines
has been grappling with a surge in inflation of late. Thus, it's crucial to delve into the factors driving inflation
in the Philippines and analyze its potential ramifications on businesses, investors, and individuals alike.

The Primary Drivers of Inflation in the Philippines

In the Philippines, both core inflation and headline inflation are influenced by a combination of factors. Core inflation, which excludes volatile items such as food and energy, reflects the underlying trend in prices and is often driven by sustained demand pressures and production costs. On the other hand, headline inflation, which includes all goods and services, is more susceptible to short-term fluctuations caused by external factors such as changes in global commodity prices and exchange rates.

One primary driver of core inflation is demand-pull inflation, where robust consumer spending and investment activities outpace the available supply of goods and services. This can be exacerbated by expansionary monetary policies aimed at stimulating economic growth. Similarly, cost-push inflation contributes to core inflation when rising production costs, such as escalating wages or import expenses, lead producers to raise prices.

Headline inflation, while influenced by similar factors, is also affected by external forces such as fluctuations in global commodity prices and exchange rates. For example, increases in oil prices can directly impact transportation and production costs, thus influencing headline inflation rates. Moreover, geopolitical events can disrupt supply chains and affect the availability and prices of imported goods, further contributing to headline inflation.

Government Measures Adressing Inflation

  • Monetary policy adjustments by the Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas (BSP)

Between May 2022 and end-2023, the Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas (BSP) tightened monetary policy aggressively by raising the policy rate ten times, from a historic low of 2.0 percent to 6.5 percent, which was deemed timely and appropriate. The effects of aggressive monetary policy tightening by the BSP are aimed at achieving macroeconomic stability by controlling inflation. However, these measures can also have adverse effects on economic growth and other aspects of the economy in the short term, requiring careful consideration and balancing by policymakers.

By providing financial support and access to essential services, these programs help alleviate the adverse effects of rising prices on the most vulnerable segments of society.
